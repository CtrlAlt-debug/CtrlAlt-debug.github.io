# Brilliant Labs Frames: A Week Into the Future  

The Brilliant Labs Frames have been out for just over a week, and even from the sidelines, they’re already looking like one of the most exciting steps forward in wearable tech in years.  

What makes them stand out isn’t just that they’re AR glasses — we’ve seen plenty of those come and go, usually with a lot of hype and very little staying power. The difference here is that the Frames are *open*. Open-source hardware, open to tinkering, open to ideas. Instead of being a polished gadget locked into some company’s walled garden, they feel more like an invitation. If you’re curious, creative, or just someone who likes to push tech beyond what it was “meant” to do, the Frames are basically a playground you can wear on your face.  

The design is another win: they look like actual glasses. No chunky headsets, no “look at me, I’m auditioning for a sci-fi extra” vibes. Just frames you could wear on the street without people pointing and whispering. That’s not a small deal — normalising wearables is half the battle, and Brilliant Labs clearly understands that subtlety beats spectacle.  

Of course, it’s early days. The software will evolve, the community will shape what’s possible, and the hardware will probably get sleeker in future versions. But even now, the Frames feel like more than a gadget launch. They’re a statement: AR doesn’t have to be locked down, overdesigned, or absurdly expensive. It can be open, playful, and human.  

I don’t have a pair (yet), but from everything I’ve seen and read, this feels like the start of something genuinely new. A week in, the Frames already look less like a curiosity and more like a foundation — and I can’t wait to see what people build on top of them.
